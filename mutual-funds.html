Stock Funds

These funds invest directly in equity or stocks. They are categorised as per the size of the company they invest in - small-, mid-, or large-cap. Large-cap companies have a high market capitalization with over $10 billion. These companies are also known as blue-chip firms. Small-cap companies have a market cap ranging from $250 million to $2 billion. Mid-cap companies have market cap between the small-cap and the large-cap companies.

Index Funds

Index funds are a type of mutual fund with a portfolio that tracks a major stock market index like S&P 500. They basically mimic the performance of a financial market index. They are passive funds, meaning, that they don't have any managers making decisions about the portfolio. This in turn translates to very low fees.

Balanced Funds

Balanced funds are a hybrid of asset classes. These can be stocks, bonds, money market instruments, and so on. The purpose of this fund is to reduce the risk of exposure across the different asset classes. Some funds have a fixed allocation strategy, meaning, that the investor has a predictable exposure to the different asset classes. Other funds allocate their funds dynamically as per the market conditions, or the investor's goals.
